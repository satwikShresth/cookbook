# Feta Butter Beans

> Spicy, warm, hearty — a salty bean soup that sticks to your ribs. Filling and cheap.

*Inspired by: https://pin.it/7JNGrDtnY*

---

## Ingredients

- 1 can large butter beans, drained
- ~1 block feta or 1 cup feta crumbles
- 1 small white onion, chopped
- 1 large tomato (or several smaller ones), roughly chopped — will be blended
- 1 tbsp garlic paste or fresh garlic, chopped
- 1–2 heaping tbsp Turkish pepper paste
- 1–2 pre-cooked sausages *(optional)*
- ½ cup chicken stock *(optional)*
- Toasted crusty bread *(highly recommended for mopping)*

### Spices

- Hot paprika *(go hard)*
- Sweet paprika *(go hard)*
- Ras el hanout *(lighter hand)*
- Ground cumin
- Garlic powder
- Onion powder

> **Note:** Do not add salt — the feta is salty enough. A small pinch of garam masala can substitute for ras el hanout, but keep it minimal; the flavor profile should stay Turkish/Greek, not Indian.

---

## Method

1. Chop onion. Blend tomato into a juice and set aside.
2. Fry onion in olive oil over medium-high heat until edges are browning and onion is turning translucent.
3. Add garlic paste and cook until raw garlic smell is gone. → [[Techniques/Garlic and Ginger Paste Base|Garlic & Ginger Paste Base]]
4. Add spices and toast briefly. → [[Techniques/Blooming Spices|Blooming Spices]]
5. Pour in tomato purée to stop the spices from burning.
6. In the blender (no need to clean it), blend the pepper paste, feta, and a splash of water or stock until smooth.
7. Once tomatoes have cooked down, add the feta-pepper mixture. You will doubt yourself here — keep going.
8. Stir in butter beans. The texture should be **thicc** — somewhere between a soup and a dip.
9. Add cooked sausage or other protein if using.
10. Serve with crusty bread.

---

## Notes

- A squeeze of lemon juice next time — it ran a little salty.
- Have a side salad; this is very hearty.
- Using roasted red peppers (as the original recipe suggests) would be simpler — just paprika, onion, and peppers.
- The beans are mild on their own, but the feta carries the whole dish.
- Also tastes great made with chickpeas.
